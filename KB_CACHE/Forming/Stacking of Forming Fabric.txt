Stacking of Forming Fabric: In multi-layer forming fabrics (Double Layer & 2.5-layer designs), stacking refers to the vertical alignment of weft yarns between the paper-side layer and the wear-side layer. Stacking happens when weft yarns in different layers sit directly above each other—not offset. In SSB (Shute Support Binder) Fabrics Two warp systems: PS warp and MS warp. So stacking can occur in: Warp direction → warp stacking and Weft direction → weft stacking. In 1.5 Layer fabric, though it is a double layer fabric, but do not have traditional double layer vertical weft stacking, but it’s MS weft sits between two top PS wefts and is the main reason to call 1.5 Layer design. Why Is Stacking Important? Stacking plays a major role in how the forming fabric performs on the machine. Good Stacking improves Drainage Uniformity, Improved Sheet Support (PS smoothness), It's one of the silent factors that separates an average fabric from a high-performance one. How to Maintain Good Stacking? Stacking is fundamentally controlled by the accurate vertical alignment of yarns across layers.This is maintained by : (a) Controlled Loom Tension and Beat-Up Force Across the Fabric Width, warp tension uniformity, weft insertion accuracy and sequence, loom beat-up consistency, Shed Change angle etc. (b) Avoid Excessive Heat-Setting Deformation During Finishing- Heat-setting stabilizes the fabric, but over-stretching or uneven heating can shift layers vertically and ruin stacking. C) Regular Process Control to Prevent Yarn Migration & Quality Inspection: Using Microscopy & Cross-Section Analysis. During weaving and Heat Setting, internal stresses may cause yarn migration for above reason, so validate stacking in weaving on loom and after heat setting.
